About David Valencia

Exploring how structured knowledge, AI systems, and commerce teams intersect.

From ecommerce operations to LLM visibility

I began in ecommerce operations, where catalog quality, merchandising, and customer questions collide in real time. That work revealed how fragile discoverability can be when the structure behind product data is inconsistent or invisible.

Today I research LLM visibility: how large language models evaluate sources, synthesize answers, and reference brands. I translate insights from that research into playbooks that help practitioners improve their data foundations and win trust inside AI-driven experiences.

AIFDS and schema-first thinking

I founded AIFDS.org to help operators, strategists, and technologists build AI-first discovery systems. We focus on schema-driven content, knowledge graphs, and experimentation frameworks that align with how assistants source information.

My work includes collaborating with teams on structured data audits, designing LLM-focused measurement, and coaching leaders on how to adapt their SEO and content programs for AI interpreters.

Currently

  • Running visibility experiments that benchmark how different models cite sources across categories.
  • Working with ecommerce leaders to translate product taxonomies into LLM-friendly knowledge.
  • Publishing research and interviews to help practitioners evolve their discovery strategies.
